CR-0010 Gross-only return disclosure

Every return figure in the document is presented gross, and no net-of-fees or net-to-investor figure appears anywhere in the 52 pages.

The document states this itself. Page 52: "As used throughout this presentation, and unless otherwise indicated, all returns are presented on a “gross” basis." And, on the same page, that gross IRRs and multiples "do not reflect management fees, “carried interest,”" — the finding notes the sentence continues that these "will reduce returns and in the aggregate are expected to be substantial."

Why the fee schedule does not cure it

Page 38 does disclose a four-tier fee schedule, a 10% hurdle and four-tier carry without catch-up, so a reader could attempt a derivation. Under the strict standard applied, this does not defeat the criterion for three reasons: a derivation the reader must perform is not a disclosed net figure; the fee basis (commitments vs drawn capital vs NAV) is not stated anywhere, so the derivation is not even well-posed; and page 42's illustration is an asset-level build-up rather than a fee and carry waterfall. The gap between the ~18-20% gross headline and any investor-level net is material and unquantifiable from this deck.

CR-0011 Predecessor fund track record unrealised

The predecessor fund cited as evidence, Neo Infra Income Opportunities Fund I (NIIOF-I), is still within its deployment phase, and its return is stated in unrealised, mark-to-model language.

Status

Page 13 describes NIIOF-I as "Fund fully committed –", with deals worth Rs 2,985 crore completed or committed, and cashflows accruing "from deals that are yet" to close. That is a fund still deploying, not harvesting.

Language

The return is labelled "Tracking gross IRR" of "~21% better than" on page 13. Page 14 states "NIIOF-I targeted gross IRR of ~18-20% p.a.; well on its path to achieving target IRRs". "Tracking" is precisely the mark-to-model formulation the criterion names. Page 17's footnote "*As per market price as on 18-02-2026" confirms three of the five completed positions are valued on marks rather than exits.

Distributions are not realisations

The distribution rates of ~12-13% for FY26 and ~8.2% in FY25 (page 13) are current yield on deployed capital, not capital returned, and do not convert the record to a realised one.

CR-0016 Valuation policy undisclosed

The document carries no valuation policy of any kind, and no mention of independent valuation.

"Valuation" itself appears four times, never as a policy: page 41, as one item on a quarterly review agenda; page 25, twice, about deal pricing negotiation with sellers; and page 44, as "value add", unrelatedly. Under the strict standard, page 41 is not a description of methodology: it says valuation is reviewed, not how it is determined, by whom, or against what standard.

The one stated basis does not reach the relevant assets

Page 17's footnote "*As per market price as on 18-02-2026" establishes a pricing source for three listed or InvIT positions. It says nothing about the unlisted operating road and solar SPVs that constitute the 80% strategy — precisely the illiquid assets this criterion targets.

Why it matters directly

The ~21% tracking gross IRR on pages 13 and 17 rests on carrying values whose derivation the document never explains, and those same unexplained marks underpin the NIIOF-II track-record claim. EY is named as Fund Auditor on page 37, but an audit is not a valuation policy, and naming an auditor does not evidence independent portfolio valuation.

CR-0014 Sector or counterparty concentration

This finding is contested. The lenient and strict evaluation passes reached opposite verdicts. The strict reading fired it and is reproduced here; both readings are set out in full in the contested-findings section, and the disagreement is deliberately not resolved.

READING A · LENIENT — does not fire

Page 23 is a dedicated guardrails page stating an explicit concentration policy: at least 80% into operating long-duration contracted-cashflow assets, and at least 80% into roads and solar. The criterion expressly provides that a stated limit defeats it. Confidence medium — what is stated are floors, not caps.

READING B · STRICT — does fire

The "Atleast 80%" statements are allocation floors that compel concentration, and reading them as a concentration policy would invert their plain meaning. A stated limit must bound exposure — no such ceiling exists in the 52 pages. Confidence high.

Counterparty overlap compounds it

Page 16 states 95% of fund exposure to entities having Central Government counterparties for NIIOF-I, page 14 names NHAI, NTPC, MORTH and SECI, and all six pipeline deals on page 25 are HAM roads. Sector concentration and counterparty concentration point the same way, unbounded by any stated cap.
