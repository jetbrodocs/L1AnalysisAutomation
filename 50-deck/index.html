L1 ANALYSIS PLATFORM 03

What it is

A pitch deck goes in. An evidenced IC memo comes out.

Five stages, each receiving every prior stage's output. The recommendation is computed deterministically from the scorecard. No model writes the verdict.

01Classify7/7 quotes verified
02Extract45/45 verified
03Diligenceregisters · $0.00
04Score17 criteria · 2 passes
05Memo12 sections + index

Scoring runs twice: a lenient pass and a strict pass. Where they disagree, the engine does not resolve it. It reports the finding as contested and prints both readings for the committee to adjudicate.

The output stands alone. No server, no login, no database. An analyst runs it on a laptop and opens the memo. A confidential PPM need never leave the building.

Six dimensions, seventeen criteria, three tiers

Governance is the largest category by design. Seven of seventeen. Operational risk predicts fund failure more strongly than financial risk, so the framework is weighted towards how allocators actually lose money.

Only the regulatory criteria are vetoes. Those are legal preconditions: a fund without valid registration cannot accept commitments at all. Everything else is weighted judgement, never a hard stop.

These are our defaults, and they are placeholders. You replace them, and promote any of them to a veto in your own set. That is not a caveat; it is the product.

L1 ANALYSIS PLATFORM 07

Why it is trustworthy

The differentiator is not the automation. It is what the engine refuses to do.

✓ Every quote is checked against the page it cites Not trusted from the model, verified in code. 102 of 105 citations matched (97.1%).
✓ Numbers must trace to an extracted value, or the run fails Numeric traceability is a hard invariant, not a quality score.
✓ The memo cannot contradict its own scorecard Enforced at the stage boundary. A memo whose verdict disagrees with its scorecard does not ship.
✓ It reports what it could not determine Section 11 is non-excludable from any export, including from the PDF an IC reads.
✓ An unreachable source is never an adverse finding Unverifiable claims never move the score, in either direction.
Vendors in this category publish hours-saved and never accuracy. That is the gap this engine is measured against. PRD 06 §1 · design posture

Benchmark context: FinanceBench found GPT-4-Turbo with retrieval incorrectly answered or refused 81% of questions over public filings. Grounding is not a nice-to-have in this domain.

L1 ANALYSIS PLATFORM 08

The co-pilot loop

57 open questions are not a failure. They are the worklist.

33 Answerable from a document we do not have. Request the PPM; many close in one action
5 Answerable by an analyst. Attest with a required source, recorded as attested, never as document-grounded
19 Externally blocked: the check could not be performed. Do not ask the manager. Routed to an owner instead

Each entry states what was searched for and where. That is what makes it a worklist and not a disclaimer. It records what has already been ruled out, so the same search is not repeated by hand.

Upload a PPM → re-run → a new version. Prior versions stay frozen and downloadable. The diff is causal:

"CR-0030 flipped because the PPM you uploaded disclosed a 2.5% GP commitment on p.14."

Ticking a box never closes a question. Only a re-run establishes what a document actually says. Otherwise an analyst could close 57 questions in 57 clicks without the engine reading a page.

L1 ANALYSIS PLATFORM 10

The system around the engine: specified, not built

Who can do what, and where a deal sits

Analyst Uploads, runs, triages, answers open questions, re-runs. The daily operator.
Super Admin Head of Research or CIO. Authors and versions the criteria that encode house policy.
IC Member Reads finished memos with full attribution to the rules that produced them. Does not upload or promote.
ODD Reviewer Risk & GRC. Reports to the COO, not the CIO. Sees no pipeline board and no stage columns at all.

ODD holds an asymmetric veto. A failing review blocks advancement into commitment. A passing review advances nothing. There is no event by which ODD moves a deal forward, because in reality there is no such power.

The power never to hire, never the power to hire. Enforced at the permission layer, not by UI convention.

Canonical allocator stages, not invented labels. The platform's centre of gravity is initial screening. It tracks the later stages so the funnel is complete, and does not pretend to run an IC.

L1 ANALYSIS PLATFORM 11

The system around the engine: specified, not built

Documents accumulate. Versions freeze. The diff explains itself.

→ Every document is addressed by its content hash The storage key is sha256(bytes); the filename is metadata, never an identifier. Two analysts forwarding the same deck produce one stored blob and one deduplication event.
→ A deal accumulates documents across vintages Fund II's deck sits alongside Fund I's, in date order, so you can see how a manager's story changed rather than only what it says today.
→ Event-sourced throughout Every state change is an auditable event with an actor and a timestamp: who uploaded what, which criteria version scored it, who exported which memo.

An open question closes one of two ways: upload a document, or attest a fact with a required source. An attested fact is labelled as attested and never rendered as a page citation. Externally-blocked questions accept neither.

v1 → v2 causal diff ILLUSTRATIVE

CR-0030 (Meaningful GP commitment) flipped from not-fired to fired because the PPM (EV-2026-000073, uploaded 24 Jul, p.14) disclosed a 2.5% GP commitment in cash. In v1 this criterion could not fire — extraction searched all 52 pages for sponsor commitment, GP commitment, co-investment and found nothing.

The diff is causal, not textual. Two memos from a non-deterministic model differ on nearly every line; a prose diff would make the tool look like it changed its mind arbitrarily. Prior versions stay frozen and downloadable.

L1 ANALYSIS PLATFORM 12

New: specified, not yet built

The information request writes itself

The memo already states, per unresolved item, what is missing and what was searched for, and every criterion carries a remediation_prompt. So the platform can assemble a ready-to-send request to the manager. An analyst reviews it and sends it.

✓ It is specific, not a blanket request Not "please send more information". The exact figure, clause or policy, named.
✓ It excludes the externally-blocked items No manager can answer a question about a register the platform could not reach. The routing already exists in the data, so the email is correct by construction rather than by the analyst remembering.

On the reference run that is 38 items to the manager (the 33 document-answerable plus 5 analyst-answerable) and 19 routed to an internal owner instead.

L1 ANALYSIS PLATFORM 14

What it cannot do yet

The limits, stated plainly

We would rather you hear these from us than find them yourselves.

01 Pre-MVP. The analysis engine works end to end; the management UI is specified, not built. Everything shown from the engine (slides 3 and 6 to 9) is real output. Everything on slides 11 to 13, including the generated information request, is a specification.
02 Two regulatory checks were unevaluated on the reference run. Regulatory verification is in build. Both are veto-tier, so that run's export carries two unevaluated vetoes. The original diagnosis (a geo-fence) was wrong: the register returns HTTP 200 to a browser user-agent and rejected only a default client string. Ordinary bot filtering, not a network block. Both checks are implementable now and are being built. Until they ship, the honest claim is document-grounded analysis with regulatory verification pending.
03 ~4% of quotes cannot be mechanically verified on column-heavy slides. They are retained and flagged quote_verified: false, never hidden. The verifier is tuned strict on purpose: a visible unverified quote beats a silently accepted fabricated one.
04 Contested findings vary between runs. Surfaced as contested with both readings printed, not smoothed over into false confidence.
L1 ANALYSIS PLATFORM 15

Where this goes next

What we are asking for

The engine is validated on one real deck. What it needs now is your rules and your decks, the two things we cannot supply ourselves.

01 A criteria workshop Half a day encoding your actual red flags, green flags and vetoes. You keep the set regardless of what follows.
02 Three decks you have already screened We run them against your criteria and you compare our memo to the conclusion you reached. That is the only benchmark that counts.
03 A pilot deal, run end to end One live deal through the loop: upload, score, route the open questions, re-run on what comes back. It is the only way to test the workflow rather than the engine.
The acceptance standard is correctness, not time saved.

If the analysis is not good enough to change an analyst's mind, no amount of management-system polish rescues it. That is why the engine was built first, and why we are showing you its output before its interface.
